Data Analytics and Business Intelligence

Data Preparation

The most underestimated task in data analysis. It can even take a lot more time than the actual modeling. Merging, shaping, and cleaning data may not sound like fun, but these are critical steps if you want to trust your findings and have a succesful analysis.

Statistical Analysis

Finding what matters and what doesn‘t. Using statistical analysis for business problems is a way of setting out the terms of how to guide decisions based on data. From statistical tests to regression models, you can learn these techniques and make better and more effective decisions.

Dashboards

Visualizing your analytics to understand your business, monitor, and take action. The key is finding a good way to let the data speak for itself. Building dashboards is about choosing chart type, layout, and presentation, while considering who the end user would be.

Learn How to Use Modern Tools

Power BI

The evolution of Excel into a powerful reporting solution. This Microsoft BI platform lets you import data from many sources, transform it, and feed it into reports and dashboards that you can easily automate and share with your team.

R Software

A free software you can use to collect data, run statistical analysis, and create graphics precisely tailored for your problem. Programming in R gives you more room to tackle complex data problems, save time, and reuse solutions.
